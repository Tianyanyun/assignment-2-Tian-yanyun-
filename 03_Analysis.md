# MRS. BULLFROG
### Author: NATHANIEL HAWTHORNE (1804-1864)

## Section 1
### Themes:
This story shows a process of Mr.Bullfrog’s perception of marriage, from a wonderful illusion to disillusionment.

### Narrative structure :

I want to divide this story into four parts.

Paragraph 1-2

This part presents Mr.Bullfrog’s attitude toward marriage, which shows his good illusion.

Paragraph 3-10

This section describes the early stage of Mr.Bullfrog and Mrs.Bullfrog’s marriage, they have a romantic and harmonious relationship.

Paragraph 11-19

This part is the climax of this story. After the accident with the carriage Mrs.Bullfrog suddenly reveals her rude, aggressive behaviour and real appearance. Mr.Bullfrog starts to doubt his choice and his wife’s true nature.

Paragraph 20-36

Mr.Bullfrog reads about the court case in the newspaper, even though he feels doubtful and emotionally conflicted. He eventually accepts his wife because of money, and he starts to accept the imperfect of marriage.

### Voice:

This story is narrated from Mr.Bullfrog’s first-person point of view.

### key passages:

Paragraph 12:

This passage shows the Mrs.Bullfrog violently striking the wagoner, Mr.Bullfrog feels very shocked, confused, and begins to feel that his wife is a stranger.

Paragraph 17:

Mrs.Bullfrog suddenly converts her attitude to wagoner, from rudeness to kindness. This dramatic change deepens Mr.Bullfrog’s doubt when he faces his wife.
